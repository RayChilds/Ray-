Top 40 Followed Instagram Influencers of 2018 (& What It Means)

The Data

I've chosen a dataset provided by Data.World. It contains the Top 40 Most Followed Instagram Influencers, their categories both general and specific, number of followers in millions, age, and nationality. The dataset omits business instagram accounts (Victoria's Secret, Nike, etc), and only explores the personalities. Something to note, these numbers themselves are subject to change at any time due to the nature of the social media world (fluctuating followers, especially in times of celebrity drama/promotion), but this would not necessarily change the influencers themselves, for reasons we'll explore below.

It's important to note most influencers play multiple roles in this multi-faceted decade. Think about musicians, for example. Back some 40 years ago, profits came predominantly from long awaited albums. With the genesis of the internet, however; most if not all of the Age of Technology has been spent fighting pirating. Napster, Limewire...etc. Lo and behold, streaming saved the day. Pirating was sharply cut back, but not without some trade-off. Revenue was cut greatly, so what's a musician to do? As a musician in the 21st century, you have to be a jack of all trades. Model, actor, instagrammer, fashion designer, perfume and makeup entreprenuers, marketeers...you name it. Conclusion

It seems to me if you have certain qualities, a perfect storm of looks and a capacity to appeal to the masses (or hire someone to do so), you're well on your way to owning a sizable portion of new wealth. Something we haven't covered here? Moo-la. Here's a factoid to blow your socks off. Ms. Kylie Jenner (our youngest instagrammer) makes one million dollars PER paid instragram post. It pays to post, ladies and gentlemen (at least if you're a Kardashian or Disney affiliate!) Would Kylie be such a rockstar if she weren't born into the already world-famous family? Chances are very, very slim. However, we do see it is possible to become famous by your own accord, as Justin Bieber started as an independent musician on YouTube. Human beings are explorers, and, seeing as we've pretty much covered exploration of land masses - the new frontier is indeed online and there certainly is a vast audience to see who will do what, next.

Further Exploration

It is no secret the United States of America plays a huge cultural role in the world, with hotly debated topics including exceptionalism, capitalism, and cultural imperialism. While exploring the data, I struggled to categorize the influencers in terms of black and white, for example, American vs. Barbadian. Is Rihanna an American influencer as she now works and lives in the United States as this is where the industry is? Can we count her as a Barbadian influencer? If ethnicity had been brought into the puzzle, perhaps all of this data could be considered skewed. To be "American" even, is a very difficult topic. What is American? Is it citizenship? It is in this particular data that we see how important the multicultural melting pot is to the American world.

Something else that occurred to me as I explored this dataset: race. Continuing on my piece above on ethnicity and nationality and "Americanism"...how much of the influencers identify as African American or mixed race? And, perhaps more importantly, how has this changed over the years? We are burdened with the limited timeline of social media, Instagram only being around for 10 years! Perhaps other means could be used to account for the top influencers of the 20th century, and how they compare to now. It will also be interesting to continue to watch the cultural infusion as the years go by.

I'd also like to do a more in-depth study on the projected values of these influencers; I'd draw on other social media applications including YouTube, Snapchat, Facebook, etc. The ages of these particular influencers struck me, what does it mean that 75% of the most followed are under the age of 30 years old? How does this impact the rest of the world? An entire study could be on the Kardashians and their cultural influence, by themselves. Disney is another piece of our puzzle that could have it's own slew of information explored in it's role on global culture, especially since it's latest aquistions of Marvel, Lucas Films, etc. If we add back in the accounts that were ommited, Nike, Nike Football, and Victoria's Secret, the values that Beauty and Soccer play in the world increase. What exactly does this mean and why? One last question on this particular food for thought, who are the demographics of this paricular app, and how are we shaping them in this increasingly digital age?
